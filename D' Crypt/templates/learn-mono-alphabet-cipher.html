Mono-alphabet Cipher


How it Works

Mono-alphabet cipher is a form of substitution cipher which makes use of random substitution. Given the letter 'A', it is replaced by a random letter from 'B' to 'Z'. There is no relationship between replacement of 'A' and replacement of 'B'.

The key can be any permutation of the 26 letters (26! = 4 x 1026 possible permutations). This makes it more difficult to break than the Shift cipher, which only has 26 possible keys.
